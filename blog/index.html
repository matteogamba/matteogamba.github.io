The toughest decision imaginable.

How Google could become a Trillion-Dollar company tomorrow — and change our world.

Google is an extremely successful company already. Yet in my opinion they are nowhere close to using their potential. As advertiser and shareholder, I find it outraging not to exploit that potential fully. As a citizen of this world, the thought that they could ever exploit this potential is frightening.

Google has been a somewhat responsible citizen thus far. But in times, when democratic governments may spy on their own population and the backlash is somewhat mild, the world should know about what Google could do — creating strongly positive economic effects for themselves, advertisers and shareholders alike but seriously shattering the foundations of democracy and privacy as we know it today.

Marketing is often thought of as creating posters and loud TV ads to capture people’s attention. Google CEO Larry Page described the profession once as “an ability to lie”. What they describe, however, is antiquated brand marketing. This is quickly getting replaced with “customer acquisition” which can be described as the “science” (not an “art”) of acquiring customers at a lower cost than their value to the business. This is the status-quo in internet marketing — be it e-commerce or services.

A simple example: Subscription websites (think Netflix or match.com) spend $100 to acquire 100 visitors to their website (paying $1 per visitor). Out of these 100 visitors, 5 become paying subscribers for US$25 each. This generates a total business value of $125 and the customer acquisition is successful.

But is it really? Notice the big inefficiency in this calculation? $95 have been spent on acquiring 95 visitors which brought $0 in value, whereas $5 have been spent on acquiring 5 visitors which brought $125 in value. This hardly sounds like efficient allocation of marketing budgets. Yet this has been the reality for the last 100 years and still is the gold standard today.

It does not have to be this way if you knew up-front who might buy your product and who will not. You could tailor your spend accordingly. Save money on those customers that will not buy your product. Increase your marketing pressure on those that are very likely to buy. A simple concept — badly understood and not implemented in any meaningful way.

Right now this concept is implemented on an extremely rudimentary level — offline companies will be as sophisticated as spending more in countries that have a higher purchasing power or spending less on TV channels with a predominantly male audience if their product is for women. Online companies will spend more for keywords with a higher margin (“Mortgage” vs. “T-Shirt”). This is nothing compared to the potential ahead. The advertising industry is getting more sophisticated every day, but right now at average e-commerce conversion rates (the ratio of visitors who buy to all visitors) of 1-5%, at least 95% of the visitors are still acquired without providing any value to the business.

The solution? Google.
Google is in a position to find out the purchase intent and purchase likelihood of any customer, including yourself before you know it.
Google knows visitors who have never purchased online — money any marketer could save.
Google knows visitors who have a 90% likelihood of purchasing your high-margin product and yet currently marketers do not spend a single dime on them.
Because you do not know what Google knows. The table below shows just how deep their access to data of individuals is and how it can be used to create a detailed profile for everyone, everywhere:

Aggregating and analyzing this kind of data volume around a single user id is clearly a humongous technological task and one which cannot be solved easily. Yet Google’s software engineers are considered the world’s leading and will master this task (if they have not already). By aggregating this wealth of data and letting big statistics make predictions, Google will know your purchase intent and likelihood to buy. But also much more.

So how exactly could Google monetize this wealth of data to become a Trillion-Dollar-Company? Currently Google monetizes one single piece of information (search queries) by allowing advertisers to show ads on the Google Search Result Page to people who searched for their brand or product (“Google Adwords”). This simple mechanism generated ad revenue for Google of around $37 billion in 2013.

However, $200 billion are spent on TV advertising worldwide, $125 billion on newspapers and magazines and $63 billion on online display ads. None of these channels is in any way efficient marketing. By using the data points from above, Google could revolutionize all of them tomorrow.

They could be the only company in the world that knows the purchasing intent and likelihood of anyone for anything.
Stated or unstated (think wedding dresses for android phone 1 which sleeps next to android phone 2 every night, which also happens to stop at Tiffany’s for 5 times in the last 2 weeks).
Wanted or unwanted. In every country. For the overwhelming majority of the world’s population.

To sum up, Google could help advertisers acquire customers much more efficiently and tailor messages and ads to every individual. This would be the end of TV and Display marketing as we know it today. This could turn Google into the most valuable company in the world. This is how big and powerful the data part of this alone is.

Yet there is a second layer here. To dominate the marketing world, you actually need to have two things: knowing purchasing intent & probability (as described earlier) and opportunities to reach as many people as possibly.

Larry Page said his vision is to develop “products that people cannot live without”. I am certain his ambitions are pure, yet where would you put ads to effectively reach people? Probably somewhere close to “products that people cannot live without”.

Effectively Google Search is such a product (When Google was down for a couple of minutes in September 2013, visitor volume of all websites dropped by 40%).
Effectively Gmail is such a product, effectively your Android phone is such a product, effectively your Chromebook is such a product, effectively YouTube is such a product.
Effectively, Google Glass could become such a product, self-driving cars could become such a product, and your digital thermostat (Nest) could become such a product.
In fact, I cannot recall one of Google’s acquisitions or products that did not give them either of the two layers: data or reach. The only other company that has one dominating “product that you cannot live without” are likely Facebook and for some parts Apple. TV and newspapers are also around, yet extremely fragmented and to be honest who will really use them 5 years out? All of this will put Google in a unique position not to be dependent on any external providers but essentially build an integrated advertising experience fully within the realm of their own products.

Many years ago, Steve Jobs was a proponent of a closed ecosystem (Mac and iOS) whilst Google was a proponent of an open ecosystem (Android). This has changed quite dramatically. Yet Jobs was merely thinking about a closed ecosystem for music or pictures. Google could become the closed ecosystem of your life.

Google, you are able to display personalized ads knowing purchase intent and probability and you have the reach to target everyone, can you please implement this solution soon? Marketing efficiency would increase dramatically, people would see relevant ads and companies could spend more funds on research & development versus wasting them in marketing. Clearly this should give our world a growth impulse. As an advertiser, I ask you: what are you waiting for?

Google, you could become the most valuable company in the world by going from a heavy dependency on search ads to owning the advertising space. You would be liked by companies (for raising their profits) and customers (for only showing personalized ads) alike. Your share price would skyrocket. As a shareholder, I ask you: when will you implement what has been long possible and stop ignoring a major growth opportunity?

Google, you could do this and our world would change completely. You know everything about us already. You could use it for us or against us. Politicians do not understand the severity of the challenge our society is facing. It is hard for the majority of the public to understand what you do. Users do not have alternatives of comparable quality to your products. You are the ultimate monopoly of data. You are the private CIA of the world.
Never before was so much power in the hands of so few. The 20th century had a moral discussion on whether it was right to do what was technically possible — using the atomic bomb. The question on whether you exploit your power could well be the moral discussion of the 21st century.

Google Chairman Eric Schmidt once said: “One day we had a conversation where we figured we could just try to predict the stock market. And then we decided it was illegal. So we stopped doing that.” Clearly Google executives will face challenges like this over and over. However, many solutions will be perfectly legal within our antiquated ‘offline’ laws. There will be substantial power in the hands of Google’s top directors to decide to either exploit that opportunity and do what is right from a shareholder’s perspective or stick by a moral ground and decide to willingly forego a $500bn business opportunity. I don’t believe there are many tougher choices out there.

Thus as a citizen of this world, I ask you to share your dilemma with us, to give users full transparency on what kind of data you know and to engage with the democratically elected bodies of the world to find a common ground.

Don’t do evil.
